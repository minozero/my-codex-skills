# Decision rules

## How to read the momentum stack

### Strong leadership
Typical profile:
- high Rank
- high R60
- high R120
- R20 high or recovering

Interpretation:
- real leadership
- can add on pullback if macro context does not invalidate the setup

### Trend intact but digesting
Typical profile:
- weak or red 1D
- R20 still high enough
- R60 / R120 remain strong
- Rank remains strong

Interpretation:
- pullback or digestion, not automatic failure

### Structural repair
Typical profile:
- 1D or 5D improved
- R20 improving from a weak base
- R60 and/or R120 stabilizing
- Rank moving from low to mid range

Interpretation:
- improving, but not leadership yet
- watch for confirmation

### Bounce only
Typical profile:
- green 1D or green 5D
- R20 low
- R60 low
- R120 low
- Rank low

Interpretation:
- reflex rally
- do not promote to leadership

### Structural breakdown
Typical profile:
- R20 collapsing
- R60 rolling over
- R120 deteriorating
- Rank falling out of leadership

Interpretation:
- sponsorship failing
- avoid or reduce

## Threshold framing

Use threshold language explicitly, but do not pretend every asset uses the same exact cutoffs.

General guide:
- R20 above ~85–90 with strong R60/R120: leadership intact
- R20 around ~55–70: improving or neutral, depends on Rank and slope
- R20 below ~40 with weak medium-term stack: fragile
- Rank below ~20 with weak R60/R120: avoid unless repair is very recent and clearly improving

## Dynamic comparison rules

Prefer:
- today vs yesterday
- today vs the last 2–3 sessions if available
- 5D and 20D vs current Rank
- price action vs momentum stack confirmation or divergence

Examples:
- price green but R20 still weak = bounce only
- price red but R20/R60/R120 still high = digestion
- 5D strong and Rank rising across sessions = improving structure
- price flat but R20 and Rank improving = quiet accumulation

## Mandatory anomaly behavior

If a datapoint is obviously malformed:
- label it as anomaly
- do not use it as the main pillar of the thesis
- do not fabricate a corrected value

## Macro interpretation guardrail

Do not label the tape as clean risk-on or risk-off unless the cross-ETF structure supports it.
If the index move and the ETF internals disagree, trust the internals more.
