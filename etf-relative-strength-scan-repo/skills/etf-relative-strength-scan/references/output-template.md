# Output template

Use this structure in this order.

## 1) Core Narrative
One sentence only.
- Lead with the single most important numeric anchor of the day.
- State what that number means for the market structure.

## 2) Most Important New Information
Split into:
- **Confirmed**
- **Reported**
- **Inferred**

State why the new information changes the probability distribution.

## 3) Three-Dimensional Structure Scan
Create a compact table for the key ETFs:
- ETF
- Price
- 5D
- 20D
- R20
- R60
- R120
- Rank
- recent R20 or Rank trend if prior sheets exist
- status label

Status labels should be explicit:
- trend intact
- trend strengthening
- trend weakening
- trend intact but digesting
- structural repair
- bounce only
- avoid
- watch for trigger
- add only if confirmed
- reduce on confirmation

## 4) Strongest Zone / Weakest Zone
Two compact tables.
Make leadership and structural failure obvious at a glance.

## 5) Theme Rotation Diagnosis
Explain the main ETF-level rotations, for example:
- semis vs software
- ex-US vs US
- ex-China EM vs China
- power / infrastructure vs nuclear beta
- energy vs broad equities
- breadth vs megacaps

## 6) False Rebound vs Real Repair Test
Mandatory.
For the most important questionable green zones, say clearly whether the move is:
- real repair
- only bounce
- trend confirmation
- still broken despite price strength

## 7) Trading-Theme Verdict
Rank the live themes:
- Tier 1 — current dominant leadership
- Tier 2 — strong secondary themes / likely expansion path
- Tier 3 — watchlist for possible repair
- Avoid / structurally weak

## 8) What You May Be Missing
Include 3–5 points.
Prioritize second-order effects, hidden internal dispersion, and misread signals.

## 9) Next-Step Validation Signals
End with a checklist of explicit triggers.
Examples:
- R20 staying above a threshold
- Rank improving or deteriorating
- semis continuing to outperform software
- ex-China EM continuing to beat China
- breadth holding despite macro stress
